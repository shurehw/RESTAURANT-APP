Purchasing Enforcement

Right product. Right vendor. Right price.
No workarounds. No wrong buys.

Most inventory systems track what was ordered. OpsOS enforces what's allowed to be ordered.

What the system enforces

  • Approved specs only — every item locked to an approved product spec. Can't substitute a cheaper cut, a different brand, or an off-list vendor.
  • Approved vendors only — each item tied to approved vendor tiers. The system won't process an order from a non-approved source.
  • Price ceilings — cost spikes detected automatically via z-score analysis. If a vendor raises a price above the ceiling, the invoice gets flagged before approval.
  • UOM consistency — pack configs, unit conversions, and base UOM enforced at the item level. No confusion between cases and eaches.

What this prevents

The $40K problem: A kitchen manager switches to a cheaper protein without telling anyone. Plate cost looks good. Guest complaints spike. Reviews drop. Revenue follows. Nobody connects the dots until it's too late.

  • Rogue purchasing — buying off-spec from unapproved vendors
  • Silent price creep — vendors raising prices without detection
  • Spec drift — gradual substitution that degrades quality
  • Invoice fraud — charges for products that don't match specs

The item catalog isn't a reference list. It's a set of rails. If it's not in the system, it doesn't get ordered. If the price is wrong, it doesn't get paid.
